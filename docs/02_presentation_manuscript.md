# Vows & Veil Presentation Manuscript

## Timing Guide

| Time | Section | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 - 0:20 | Greeting | Open confidently and greet the panel |
| 0:20 - 0:50 | Introduction | State the project name and purpose |
| 0:50 - 1:30 | Problem | Explain the wedding-planning problem being solved |
| 1:30 - 2:20 | Website rationale | Describe why the site was designed this way |
| 2:20 - 3:10 | Target users and content | Identify who the site is for and what they need |

## Manuscript

**[0:00 - 0:20 | Greeting]**

Good day, professors and classmates. We are presenting our website, **Vows & Veil**, a wedding planning studio website designed to help couples plan elegant celebrations with less stress.

**[0:20 - 0:50 | Introduction]**

Our project focuses on creating a refined and organized online presence for a wedding planning business. The website introduces the studio, its services, venue guidance, gallery, and contact information in one connected experience.

**[0:50 - 1:30 | Problem]**

The main problem we wanted to solve is that wedding planning can feel overwhelming. Couples often need help with budgeting, venue decisions, styling, and coordination, but they may not know where to start. This website gives them a clear first step and presents the business in a calm, professional way.

**[1:30 - 2:20 | Website Rationale]**

We chose this concept because weddings need both emotion and structure. The site reflects that balance through soft visuals, organized content blocks, and clear calls to action. The home page introduces the brand, the services page explains support levels, the venues page helps with decision-making, the gallery page shows inspiration, and the contact page makes inquiry simple.

**[2:20 - 3:10 | Target Users]**

Our main users are engaged couples, families helping with planning, and clients who want a wedding that feels polished and well-managed. We also designed the website so it can guide people who are still exploring options, especially those comparing venues, budgets, and service packages.

**[3:10 - 4:10 | Frontend Design and UI/UX]**

For the frontend, we used a luxury editorial style with warm neutral colors, elegant typography, and card-based layouts. The interface is designed to feel premium but still easy to read. We used clear section headers, visual hierarchy, buttons with strong contrast, and a layout that keeps the most important information easy to scan. The gallery and budget calculator also add interaction without making the website feel cluttered.

**[4:10 - 4:40 | Responsiveness]**

We also made the website responsive so it can adjust to smaller screens. The grid layouts stack on mobile, the sidebar navigation becomes a mobile drawer, and content spacing remains readable across different screen sizes. This helps the website stay usable on phones, tablets, and desktops.

**[4:40 - 5:00 | Conclusion and Closing]**

Overall, Vows & Veil is a website that combines presentation, usability, and a calm visual identity for wedding planning. It is meant to guide users from inspiration to inquiry in a simple and elegant way. Thank you for listening. We are ready for your questions.

## Speaker Cues

Speak slowly during the problem statement so the panel can understand the purpose clearly. Emphasize the phrases **calm**, **organized**, and **responsive** because they describe the core design idea.

Pause briefly after the website name, after the problem statement, and before the closing line. Those pauses make the presentation sound more confident and natural.

## Transition Lines

Use these lines if you want smoother delivery:

"With that purpose in mind, we designed the site to feel both elegant and practical."

"From there, we organized the pages around the parts couples usually ask about first."

"To make the site usable on different devices, we also adjusted the layout responsively."

## Emphasis Suggestions

Stress the words **wedding planning**, **calm**, **clear**, **responsive**, and **inquiry**.

When you mention the design, slow down a little on **warm neutral colors** and **elegant typography** so the judges hear the visual direction clearly.

## Pause Suggestions

Pause after "Good day, professors and classmates."

Pause after "The main problem we wanted to solve is that wedding planning can feel overwhelming."

Pause before the closing line so the ending sounds deliberate instead of rushed.

## Backup Lines for Nervous Presenters

"Our website helps couples plan weddings in a more organized way."

"We designed the pages to guide users from inspiration to contact."

"The layout is responsive, so it still works well on smaller screens."

## Suggested Gestures and Body Language

Stand upright and keep your hands relaxed at waist level.

Point lightly toward screenshots when explaining the home page, services page, gallery, and contact page.

Nod slightly when moving from the problem to the solution so the presentation feels structured.
