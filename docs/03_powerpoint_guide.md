# Vows & Veil PowerPoint Creation Guide

## Recommended Deck Size

Use **7 slides**. That is enough to explain the website clearly without overcrowding the presentation.

## Recommended Presentation Flow

1. Title slide
2. Problem and project concept
3. Homepage and overall design system
4. Services and venue guidance
5. Budget calculator and practical planning tools
6. Gallery and contact page
7. Closing slide

## Suggested Slide Titles

1. `Vows & Veil`
2. `Why We Built This Website`
3. `Homepage Experience`
4. `Services and Venue Guidance`
5. `Planning Tools`
6. `Gallery and Contact`
7. `Thank You`

## Slide-by-Slide Guide

### Slide 1: Title Slide

Place the website name, project members, subject, and section.

Use one strong screenshot from the hero section or a clean crop of the homepage banner.

Keep the layout minimal with a large title and one short subtitle.

Suggested opening line: "Good day, we are presenting Vows & Veil, a wedding planning website focused on elegance and clarity."

### Slide 2: Problem and Project Concept

Show the problem the website solves: wedding planning is stressful, scattered, and hard to organize.

Use a simple two-column layout with "Problem" on one side and "Solution" on the other.

Add one short statement about helping couples plan with less confusion.

### Slide 3: Homepage and Overall Design System

Show screenshots of the home hero, editorial cards, and signature banner.

Highlight the warm cream and gold palette, the serif script headings, and the clean card-based layout.

Explain that the homepage sets the mood while also giving users direct next steps.

### Slide 4: Services and Venue Guidance

Use screenshots from the services page and venues page.

Highlight how the site helps users compare planning levels and venue types.

Show the package cards and venue cards so the panel can see the structured information design.

### Slide 5: Planning Tools

Show the budget calculator section from the home page.

If possible, include a screenshot that shows the inputs and the resulting budget table.

Explain that the tool helps users think about guest count, venue style, and planning priorities in a more organized way.

### Slide 6: Gallery and Contact

Show the gallery grid, one lightbox screenshot, and the contact page form.

Highlight the gallery filter chips, the modal viewer, and the inquiry form with labeled fields.

Explain that this part supports both inspiration and action.

### Slide 7: Thank You

Use a simple closing slide with the logo or brand mark and a short thank-you message.

Add the team names in small text below the title.

Keep this slide uncluttered so it feels polished and confident.

## Suggested Screenshots

Homepage hero with the video background.

Budget calculator inputs and table.

Services page package cards.

Gallery page with the filter chips and one open lightbox.

Contact page form and direct contact details.

## Best Sections to Showcase

Homepage hero because it shows the visual identity immediately.

Budget calculator because it shows practical functionality.

Gallery because it shows interaction and content structure.

Contact because it shows usability and real-world purpose.

## UI/UX Elements Worth Highlighting

Clear call-to-action buttons.

Readable hierarchy in headings and cards.

Consistent spacing and rounded card styling.

Responsive stacking on smaller screens.

Accessible form labels and status feedback.

Gallery controls that make browsing easier.

## How to Explain Responsiveness Visually

Show one desktop screenshot and one mobile screenshot if possible.

Point out how the sidebar becomes a mobile drawer, how the grids stack, and how the calculator table remains usable.

If you cannot show mobile screenshots, explain the breakpoint behavior using one slide with a simple device mockup.

## Recommended Animations and Transitions

Use very light fade or dissolve transitions.

Avoid flashy motion because the website itself already has a calm, editorial tone.

Use builds only when revealing screenshots one at a time.

## Layout Recommendations

Use generous margins and consistent alignment.

Keep one main idea per slide.

Do not overcrowd slides with long paragraphs.

## Typography Recommendations

Use a serif headline font for elegance and a clean sans-serif font for body text.

Match the website style by using warm, refined typography instead of heavy corporate fonts.

Suggested pairings: `Cormorant Garamond` style headings with a simple sans-serif body font.

## Color Palette Suggestions

Use cream, beige, soft gold, muted blush, and deep brown accents.

Keep the slide background light so screenshots and text remain readable.

Match the website rather than introducing bright unrelated colors.

## Design Consistency Tips

Reuse the same button shape, card radius, and palette across the deck.

Keep headings aligned and avoid mixing too many visual styles.

Use the brand mark consistently on the title and closing slides.

## Minimalist Presentation Strategy

If you are unsure, remove more content instead of adding more.

One strong screenshot with one short explanation is better than three crowded screenshots.

The site already communicates elegance, so the deck should support that feeling rather than compete with it.

## Technical Presentation Best Practices

Mention only the frontend topics the panel can verify in the source: layout, responsiveness, forms, gallery behavior, and visual hierarchy.

Be ready to explain why the site uses cards, grids, a sidebar, and a hero video.

If asked about interactivity, explain it as supporting usability rather than decoration.

## Presenter Tips

Keep eye contact after each key point.

Project your voice slightly more when you say the project name and the problem statement.

Do not read every word from the slide.

## How to Answer Difficult Instructor Questions

Return to the design rationale.

Explain that each UI choice supports calm planning, easier scanning, and faster decision-making.

If you do not know a detail, say that the site was designed to prioritize clarity, and then point to the relevant section.

## Common PowerPoint Mistakes

Too much text on one slide.

Using unrelated stock templates that do not match the website.

Adding too many animations.

Showing screenshots that are too small to understand.

Using colors that clash with the project palette.

## What Not to Put on Slides

Long code blocks.

Paragraphs copied from the manuscript.

Random decorative icons that do not match the brand.

Busy backgrounds that make screenshots harder to see.

## Recommended Visual Hierarchy

Slide title first.

Screenshot second.

One short explanatory line third.

Small footer text last.

## Recommended Text Limits Per Slide

Title: 4 to 8 words.

Subtitle: 1 short sentence.

Body text: 2 to 4 lines only.

## Suggested Theme Direction

Choose a modern editorial or luxury wedding theme.

In Canva or PowerPoint, look for templates with soft neutrals, elegant serif headings, and clean spacing.

## Suggested Opening Slide Design

Large centered title.

Subtle background image or faded hero screenshot.

Small team line at the bottom.

## Suggested Ending Slide Design

Simple thank-you message.

Brand mark or logo centered.

Very little extra text.
